📖 Quick Explainer

How do ad networks actually pay you? Most use one of three models:

CPM

Pay per 1,000 impressions. Best for high-traffic sites.

CPC

Pay per click. Best when your audience is highly engaged.

CPA

Pay per action (signup, sale). Higher payouts but rarer for blogs.

In 2025, top networks use programmatic advertising — ads are auctioned in real-time via header bidding, meaning multiple advertisers compete for your inventory, driving your earnings up automatically.

Google AdSense

Beginner Friendly

The gold standard for blog monetization and the most widely used ad network in the world. AdSense automatically matches display, video, and text ads to your content and audience — and even has an Auto Ads feature that uses AI to find the optimal placement positions on your site without you lifting a finger.

It's not always the highest-paying option, but its reliability, ease of use, and trust factor make it the natural starting point for most bloggers. High-value niches like finance, technology, and health tend to see the best CPC rates.

Mediavine

Established Blogs

Mediavine is a beloved ad management platform among lifestyle bloggers, food writers, and travel creators. It was actually founded by bloggers, which shows in its creator-first policies. It's known for its fast ad delivery (meaning faster page loads and better user experience), a large advertiser pool, and consistently strong RPMs.

Publishers who switch from Ezoic to Mediavine commonly report RPM increases of 50–80% in their first month. Mediavine keeps 25% and passes 75% of revenue directly to you — and they're transparent about it.

Ezoic

AI-Powered

Ezoic uses machine learning to continuously test thousands of ad combinations — placements, sizes, formats — and finds the layout that generates the most revenue for your specific audience. It's widely considered the best stepping stone between AdSense and premium networks like Mediavine.

Ezoic has removed its 10,000 pageview requirement — sites under that threshold can still join through the "Access Now" program. It also gives access to Google AdX inventory (premium advertisers not available through standard AdSense). Bonus: Ezoic includes free tools for site speed (Leap) and keyword research (NicheIQ).

Monetag

Global Traffic

Monetag (formerly PropellerAds' sister platform) is one of the few networks that genuinely monetizes traffic from Southeast Asia, Latin America, and other regions that most premium networks ignore. If a significant chunk of your audience comes from non-Tier-1 countries, Monetag is often your best option.

Its push notification format tends to outperform other formats, with reported RPMs of around $2–$5 — modest, but often higher than AdSense on the same traffic. It also boasts a 100% fill rate, so you'll never have empty ad slots.

Sovrn

Low Payout Threshold

Sovrn is an ad tech and data monetization company that gives publishers access to header bidding, display ads, and even email monetization — a rare offering. It's a versatile platform suited to bloggers who want more control over their ad stack rather than a fully managed solution.

One of Sovrn's standout features is its low minimum payout of just $25, making it practical for smaller publishers who don't want to wait months to see their earnings. It also offers solid audience behavior analytics alongside standard ad performance data.

🗺️ Which Network Is Right For You?
Just starting out (under 10K pageviews)

Start with Google AdSense + Infolinks (they don't conflict). Add Skimlinks or Amazon Associates if you review products. Use Adsterra or Monetag for additional non-overlapping formats.

Growing blog (10K–50K pageviews)

Switch to Ezoic — it typically outperforms AdSense significantly at this stage. Keep Amazon Associates running alongside it.

Established blog (50K+ sessions/mo)

Apply to Mediavine (lifestyle) or Raptive (100K+ pageviews). These offer the highest RPMs in the industry for qualifying publishers.

Non-Tier-1 traffic (Asia, Latin America, etc.)

Monetag, PropellerAds, and Adsterra are your best bets — they actively monetize global traffic that premium networks largely ignore.
