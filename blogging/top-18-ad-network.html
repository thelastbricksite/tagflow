1. Google AdSense

Easiest to Start No Min Traffic

Google AdSense remains the world's largest ad network and the starting point for almost every monetizing blogger. It's free to join, has no minimum traffic requirement, and displays contextual ads automatically matched to your content. The barrier to entry is low — you need original content, a clean site, and compliance with Google's policies.

The honest truth about AdSense in 2026: RPMs have improved but are still modest. Bloggers in high-value niches (finance, legal, tech) can earn $6–$15 RPM, while general lifestyle bloggers see $2–$4 RPM. The biggest advantage is Google's massive advertiser pool, which means your inventory is always filled.

💡 Pro Tip AdSense is best used as a starting point. Once you hit 10,000+ monthly pageviews, test Ezoic alongside it. Once you hit 50K sessions, apply to Mediavine. AdSense is the floor, not the ceiling.

2. Ezoic

AI-Powered No Min Traffic

Ezoic is an AI-powered ad platform that uses machine learning to test thousands of ad placements, sizes and combinations to find what earns the most for your specific audience. It connects to Google AdSense and adds premium demand on top. Most bloggers see a 50–200% RPM improvement over AdSense alone when switching to Ezoic.

In 2026, Ezoic removed their 10,000 pageview minimum — anyone can join. Their free tier works well for small blogs, and their premium tier (requiring application) offers access to higher-paying demand partners. The setup takes about 2 hours but pays off quickly.

ℹ️ Important Ezoic requires installing their script or using their Cloudflare integration. Page speed can drop slightly — use their Site Speed Accelerator tool to offset this.

3. Mediavine

50K Sessions/mo Highest RPM

Mediavine is the gold standard for mid-tier bloggers. Known for excellent publisher support, transparent reporting, and consistently high RPMs, it's the network most bloggers aspire to when starting out. Their 50,000 sessions/month threshold (sessions, not pageviews) is achievable within 6–12 months for a growing blog.

Mediavine is creator-owned and deeply supportive of the blogging community. RPMs average $15–$40, with food, parenting, travel and lifestyle niches performing especially well. They share 75% of revenue with publishers — one of the highest rates in the industry.

💡 The Mediavine Path Start on AdSense → Switch to Ezoic at 10K pageviews → Apply to Mediavine at 50K sessions. This is the standard path most successful bloggers take.

4. AdThrive / Raptive

100K PV/mo Highest RPMs

AdThrive (rebranded to Raptive in 2023) is considered the most prestigious ad network for bloggers and the highest-paying at scale. Requiring 100,000 monthly pageviews and a primarily US audience, it serves top-tier food, parenting, travel and home bloggers. RPMs of $20–$50+ are common for established sites.

They offer a 75% revenue share, dedicated account management, and premium advertiser relationships. If you're at 100K pageviews, applying to Raptive before Mediavine is worth doing as they typically pay 10–30% more for the same traffic.

5. Monumetric

10K PV/mo Good Mid-Tier

Monumetric (formerly The Blogger Network) sits between Ezoic and Mediavine in both requirements and earnings. They require 10,000 monthly pageviews and offer a managed service where their team handles ad placement optimization for you. RPMs of $5–$12 are typical, with travel, finance and lifestyle blogs performing best.

One unique feature: they offer a $99 setup fee for blogs between 10,000–80,000 monthly pageviews (waived at 80K+). This is worth paying given the RPM improvement over AdSense alone.

6. Media.net

Yahoo/Bing Ads Best AdSense Alt

Media.net is powered by the Yahoo/Bing contextual ad network — making it the strongest direct alternative to Google AdSense. It serves ads that look very similar to AdSense but pulls from Microsoft's advertiser pool, which means higher CPCs for certain niches especially finance, business and tech.

Many publishers run Media.net alongside AdSense using different ad slots, effectively doubling their monetization. Approval requires English-language content and primarily US/UK/Canadian traffic. RPMs are competitive at $3–$10 for qualifying traffic.

7. PropellerAds

Instant Approval Global Traffic

PropellerAds is one of the easiest networks to join with instant approval and no minimum traffic. They specialise in push notifications, interstitial ads and native ads — formats that work well for entertainment, gaming and download niches. RPMs are lower ($1–$5) but approval is open to sites with non-English and developing country traffic that other networks decline.

Best for: new bloggers who need something while waiting for AdSense approval, sites with global or Asian/African traffic, and entertainment niches where mainstream networks don't perform well.

8. Infolinks

In-Text Ads Use Alongside AdSense

Infolinks specialises in in-text ads — keywords in your content become highlighted links that show ads on hover or click. This format doesn't compete with display ad space, making Infolinks ideal for running alongside AdSense or Ezoic to add incremental revenue without affecting existing ad slots.

RPMs are low ($0.50–$3) but the additive nature makes it worthwhile for text-heavy blogs. They also offer display and interstitial formats. Approval is very easy — most sites are approved within 48 hours.

9. Amazon Native Shopping Ads

Amazon Associates Great for Product Blogs

Amazon Native Shopping Ads automatically display relevant Amazon products on your pages — combining the visual appeal of display ads with affiliate commissions. Unlike standard display ads, you earn a commission when readers click through and buy anything on Amazon within 24 hours, not just the displayed product.

Best for: product review blogs, how-to sites with product recommendations, tech and gadget blogs. Commission rates vary by category (1–10%) but the high conversion rate of Amazon makes this one of the most effective monetization methods for the right niche.

They require 20,000 monthly pageviews and primarily US-based English content. Their community aspect and editorial opportunities are an added bonus beyond the ad revenue.

A/B Testing 10K PV/mo

They require a minimum of 10,000 monthly pageviews and a $50 payout minimum. Best for bloggers who want a hands-off optimization approach — their team manages the testing and configuration.

RPMs of $6–$18 are typical, with European traffic performing particularly well. Their setup is fully managed with dedicated account support and fast integration via a simple script.

15. Revcontent

Native Content Ads 50K PV/mo

Revcontent is a content discovery and native advertising network — similar to Taboola but with higher quality standards and better RPMs for qualifying publishers. Their "recommended content" widgets appear at the bottom of articles and blend naturally with editorial content, achieving high click-through rates without disrupting the reading experience.

They are selective in their approval process (requiring 50,000 monthly pageviews and quality content) but offer RPMs of $3–$10 for US traffic — often outperforming standard display ads in news and entertainment niches.

16. Taboola

Large Publishers 500K PV/mo

Taboola is one of the world's largest content discovery platforms, powering the "recommended content" sections on major news sites including USA Today, BBC and Bloomberg. For publishers who qualify, their demand pool is enormous and RPMs are solid for high-volume news and entertainment sites.

The 500,000 pageview minimum makes Taboola out of reach for most bloggers, but for news sites and high-traffic publications it's a significant revenue stream. Their exclusive partnerships with major publishers mean premium advertiser access.

17. Outbrain

Enterprise Only 10M PV/mo

Outbrain is Taboola's main competitor in the content discovery space and is essentially enterprise-only with a 10 million pageview/month minimum. It powers recommended content on CNN, The Guardian, Le Monde and hundreds of premium publishers. Included here for completeness — most bloggers won't qualify for years.

If you're at this traffic level, Outbrain and Taboola together can add significant revenue as a supplement to your primary display ad network. RPMs of $2–$8 on volume this large translates to substantial monthly earnings.

18. Sovrn //Commerce

Affiliate + Display No Min Traffic

Sovrn //Commerce (formerly VigLink) automatically converts product mentions in your content into affiliate links — requiring no manual effort. It scans your articles for brand and product names, then adds affiliate tracking to links you've already written. Combined with their display ad offering, it provides a dual revenue stream.

Best for product-heavy blogs, review sites and blogs that mention brands frequently. Commission rates vary by merchant but the automation is the major selling point — you simply install a script and existing content starts earning.

Which Ad Network Is Right for You?
You're just starting (under 10K pageviews/month)

→ Start with Google AdSense + Ezoic (free tier). Add Infolinks for incremental in-text revenue.

You have 10K–50K pageviews/month

→ Switch to Ezoic premium or Monumetric. Add Amazon Native Ads if you review products.

You have 50K–100K pageviews/month

→ Apply to Mediavine immediately. This is the single most impactful upgrade a growing blogger can make — RPMs typically 3–5× better than AdSense.

You have 100K+ pageviews/month

→ Apply to Raptive (AdThrive) for the best RPMs at scale. Add Revcontent or Taboola for additional native ad revenue.

Your traffic is non-English or from developing countries

→ PropellerAds + Ezoic are the best options. Most premium networks require primarily US/UK/AU traffic to achieve high RPMs.

The 2026 bottom line: Don't stay on AdSense forever. The path is clear — AdSense → Ezoic → Monumetric → Mediavine → Raptive. Each step significantly increases your RPM for the same traffic. The bloggers earning $3,000–$10,000/month from display ads aren't getting magical traffic — they're just on the right network for their traffic level.
